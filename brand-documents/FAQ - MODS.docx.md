With the new vision does the team have any concerns with the war in the Middle East and the real estate market in Dubai?  
Dubai’s real estate market has been through multiple major shocks over the past two decades: the 2008 global financial crisis (prices dropped 50%, recovered fully), the 2014-2019 oil price correction (25-30% decline, recovered), and COVID (brief disruption, full recovery within 12-18 months). 

In every case, the market came back, stronger.

We’re monitoring the situation closely. The recent escalation has caused short-term transaction slowdowns and market uncertainty, that’s real and we won’t downplay it. 

But Dubai recorded $250B in real estate transactions in 2025, its highest year ever. The city’s 90% expat population, rental demand, and golden visa programme continue to underpin the fundamentals.

Every market cycle has the same pattern. Uncertainty hits, retail sells at a loss or freezes, while smart money capital positions itself.
