6. Brand Voice
Epic’s voice blends the credibility of a private bank with the energy and directness of a fintech built for crypto-native users. The reference points are Monzo and Revolut: friendly, human, clear, and confident without being stiff or corporate.

6.1 Voice Characteristics

Confident:
We state what we’ve done and what we offer without hedging. When we say 8%+ yield, we explain exactly where it comes from. That specificity is the source of our confidence.

Direct:
We don’t pad sentences. We get to the number, the benefit, or the action within the first line. If a section can be said in 20 words, we don’t use 40.

Data-backed:
Every claim has a number behind it. 8% yield. 10% appreciation. $20M raised. 100+ clients. We lead with proof, not promises.

Warm and human:
We sound like a real person who happens to be extremely good at their job. We use “you” and “we” naturally. We explain complex things simply because we respect the reader’s time, not because we think they’re uninformed.

Crypto-native without being exclusive:
We assume the reader knows what USDC, APY, and RWAs are. We don’t explain basic crypto concepts. But we do explain anything specific to our products or our market (like why Dubai real estate specifically, or how our off-ramp rails work).

8. Content Guidelines
Writing Rules

1. Lead with the number.
8%, 10%, $20M, 100+ clients, 150 countries. Proof points come first, always. If a sentence doesn’t have a specific claim or data point, ask whether it needs to exist.

2. Back every claim with a comparator.
Don’t just say “8% yield.” Say “8% yield vs. 3–4% from stablecoin farms.” Context makes the number meaningful.

3. Keep copy action-oriented.
CTAs should tell the user exactly what happens next: “Off-ramp now,” “Start earning,” “See loan options.” Avoid generic labels like “Learn more” or “Submit.”

4. Acknowledge the heritage, don’t dwell on it.
Ethernity, Messi, Space Force, Shaq: these are credibility markers, not the brand identity. Use them to reinforce trust, not to tell the origin story every time.

5. Explain where the yield comes from.
Every mention of 8%+ yield should be tied to Dubai real estate and tenant rent. Never leave yield claims floating without context.

6. Write for humans, not search engines.
SEO keywords should be woven in naturally. If a sentence sounds awkward because of keyword placement, rewrite it. The reader always comes first.

7. One idea per section.
Don’t try to say everything everywhere. Each section of a page, each social post, each email should advance one clear point.

9. Brand Story
Founding Story
Epic started as Ethernity Chain in January 2021, founded by Nick Rose Ntertsas. Nick bought 500 Bitcoin at a Las Vegas poker game in 2011, later became Chief Digital Officer at Global Wildlife Conservation, and went viral with an Amazon fires Instagram post shared by Leonardo DiCaprio and Kim Kardashian.

Ethernity became one of the most visible NFT platforms of the 2021 cycle. The Messi collection generated $3.2M in its first hour. Partnerships with the U.S. Space Force, Shaquille O’Neal, and Toys R Us followed. The team raised $20M from Ripple, Algorand, Polygon, Eric Schmidt’s Steel Perlot, and Michael Rubin.

As the NFT market cooled, the team recognised that their institutional network, execution capability, and capital were better deployed solving a more durable problem: the $300B opportunity in deploying Web3 treasury capital into income-generating real-world assets.

The Problem That Sparked the Pivot
Crypto-wealthy individuals and protocol treasuries were sitting on massive stablecoin positions with nowhere productive to put them. Traditional banks didn’t understand crypto collateral. Yield farms returned 3–4% with no appreciation. Off-ramping to local currencies was expensive and slow. There was no single service that solved all of these problems together.

Long-Term Vision
To become the default financial operating system for the on-chain wealthy. A world where every major Web3 treasury allocates a portion to Epic’s RWA stack, and every crypto HNWI from the Middle East to South Asia to Europe uses Epic to earn yield, spend, borrow, and manage wealth with the ease of a private bank and none of the gatekeeping.
